What It Is

The Professionals' Major

The PGA Championship is one of golf's four major championships. Held in May, it was historically in August but moved to May in 2019 to avoid conflicts with other sports and to position itself as a spring major.

The PGA Championship is unique in that it moves to different courses each year, keeping the tournament fresh and preventing any single course from dominating the championship's narrative.

Why it matters: Winning the PGA Championship completes Grand Slam quest for golfers. Tiger Woods's five PGA Championships. Rory McIlroy's four. These define legacies.

Format

Tournament Structure

Rotating Venues

Unlike the Masters (at Augusta) or U.S. Open (USGA courses), the PGA Championship moves annually. This prevents familiarity and keeps the course setup fresh.

72-Hole Format

Four rounds of 18 holes. Lowest score wins. Tiebreakers are resolved in sudden-death playoff.

Evolution

Recently moved from August to May. Originally held at PGA member courses, now open to any prestigious course. The championship evolves constantly.

Culture

PGA Championship Moments

Tiger's Five Wins: Established dominance with multiple PGA Championships throughout his career.

Rory's Resurgence (2024): Won his fourth major, establishing himself as a consistent champion.

Collin Morikawa's Breakthrough (2021): Young player winning a major at Kiawah Island.

Format Change Impact: Moving from August to May changed the tournament's place in the golf calendar and professional schedules.
