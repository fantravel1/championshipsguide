What It Is

The World's Longest Match

The Snooker World Championship is professional snooker's most prestigious tournament. Held annually at The Crucible in Sheffield since 1977, the final is one of sport's longest competitions.

The final is a best-of-35 frames match, meaning up to 35 individual games must be played. Combined with earlier rounds, tournaments last 17 days of continuous play.

Why it matters: Snooker's World Championship is historic and prestigious. Ronnie O'Sullivan's recent dominance. Stephen Hendry's six consecutive titles in the 1990s. These victories define careers.

Format

Tournament Structure

Longest Match in Sport

The final is best-of-35 frames. Each frame is a complete game. Best-of-35 means up to 35 frames must be played. The finals last days with multiple matches per day.

Early Rounds

Earlier rounds are best-of-19, best-of-17, best-of-13. The tournament narrows the field from 128 players to 2 finalists.

The Crucible Tradition

Played at The Crucible Theatre in Sheffield. Same venue since 1977. The venue is sacred in snooker culture.
