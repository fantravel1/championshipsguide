What It Is

The Greatest Men's Tennis Championship

Wimbledon Men's Singles is tennis's most prestigious men's championship. Held every June at the All England Club on grass courts, it is the oldest Grand Slam championship still played at its original location.

The grass courts at Wimbledon are unique. Fast, unpredictable, and challenging. Only players with grass court technique can dominate.

Why it matters: Winning Wimbledon Men's Singles is the pinnacle of men's tennis. Roger Federer's eight Wimbledon titles define his legacy. Novak Djokovic's four titles validate grass court mastery. One Wimbledon title elevates a career.

Format

Championship Structure

128 Men Drawing

128 men in singles draw. Best-of-five-set format. Matches can last 4-5+ hours.

Grass Court Tennis

Fast courts favor serve-and-volley players. Big servers dominate. Baseline rallies are shorter than on clay.

Centre Court Magic

Finals are played on Centre Court, the most iconic court in tennis. Strawberries and cream tradition continues.
