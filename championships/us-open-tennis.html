What It Is

Tennis's Year-Ending Grand Slam

The U.S. Open is one of tennis's four Grand Slam championships. Held every September in New York City, it is the final major of the calendar year. Players arrive battle-tested after eight months of competition.

The U.S. Open is famous for its passionate American crowds, hard courts, and dramatic matches. Arthur Ashe Stadium is one of tennis's iconic venues.

Why it matters: Winning the U.S. Open caps off a year with a Grand Slam. For Americans, winning at home carries extra significance.

Tournament Structure

Hard Court Excellence

Played on DecoTurf hard courts at Billie Jean King National Tennis Center. Fast courts favor aggressive play and big servers.

Draw & Format

128 singles players. Men play best-of-five sets. Women play best-of-three sets. Two weeks of matches from first round to finals.
