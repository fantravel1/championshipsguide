What It Is

The Ultimate Clay Court Test

The French Open is one of tennis's four Grand Slam championships. Held in May-June in Paris on red clay courts, it is the most physically demanding Grand Slam.

Clay court tennis is slower, requires sliding and repositioning, and demands extreme endurance. Matches are long. Players lose fluids, energy, and sometimes their minds.

Why it matters: Winning the French Open makes you a clay court legend. Rafael Nadal's 14 French Open titles define his legacy. He is the undisputed king of clay. The French Open is his trophy.

Format

The Tournament Structure

Clay Court Dynamics

Clay slows the ball. Rallies are longer. Points take more exchanges. Serve-and-volley is less effective. Baseline players dominate.

Match Format

Men: Best-of-five sets. Women: Best-of-three sets. Matches last 3-6+ hours regularly. The French Open's matches are longest in tennis.

The Grind

Two weeks of matches on clay. Players' bodies suffer. Recovery is difficult. Mental toughness is tested.

The Final

A best-of-five-set match (men) or best-of-three (women). Can last 4-5+ hours. The champion is declared clay court royalty.

Travel

Attending the French Open

Ticket Difficulty: Very Hard

French Open tickets are competitive. Expect $50-$800+ depending on match. Finals cost $500-$2,000+. Court assignments are unknown until day-of, making planning difficult.

Day tickets are slightly more accessible than reserved seating. Outer courts are easier to access than Centre Court.
