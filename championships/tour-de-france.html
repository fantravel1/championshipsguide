What It Is

The World's Hardest Sporting Event

The Tour de France is an annual cycling race held primarily in France. For 21 consecutive days in July, elite cyclists compete across 21 stages spanning 2,100+ miles of varied terrain.

Riders climb the Alps and the Pyrenees ‚Äî mountains so steep that cyclists walk. They ride through valleys, through cities, along coasts. Only the strongest riders finish. Many don't.

Why it matters: Winning the Tour de France is considered the greatest achievement in cycling. Lance Armstrong's seven victories (later stripped) became his identity. Eddy Merckx's five wins defined his legendary status. The yellow jersey ‚Äî worn by the race leader ‚Äî is the most iconic garment in cycling.

Format

The Tour de France Format

21 Stages Over 23 Days

Each stage is a different route. Some are flat (sprinters' days). Some have mountains (climbers dominate). Some are time trials (individuals race against the clock). Total distance: 2,100-2,300 miles.

The Yellow Jersey

After each stage, the rider with the lowest cumulative time wears the yellow jersey the next day. Gain time on rivals through faster rides or bonuses. Lose time to rivals through punctures or crashes.

The Mountains

Multiple stages include climbs to mountain peaks. The Col du Tourmalet (6,939 feet), the Mont Blanc loop, the Alpe d'Huez ‚Äî these are legendary. Riders grind uphill at walking pace. Thousands of fans line the mountains, screaming encouragement.

Culture

Tour de France Traditions

The Peloton: The main group of riders travels together, drafting and conserving energy. Breaks form when riders attack. The pace varies dramatically.

Fan Support: Millions line the roads in France. Fans paint messages on the road. They ring bells, wave flags, create a sea of noise. It's a national celebration of cycling.

Iconic Moments: Greg LeMond's 1989 comeback win on the final time trial. Miguel Indurain's five consecutive victories (1991-1995) establishing dominance. Chris Froome's four victories establishing a new era. These moments define cycling.

The Suffering: Unlike other sports, the Tour is about endurance and suffering. Riders lose 5-10 pounds over three weeks. They crash. They bonk. They keep pedaling. The race is a test of will.

Travel

Spectating the Tour de France

Ticket Difficulty: Easy to Moderate

Unlike other championships, the Tour de France is free to watch. Fans line the roads and mountains without paying. Many stages pass through small towns where crowds gather spontaneously.

If you want premium seating in major cities (Paris, mountains, finishes), you can purchase tickets. Mountain stages are the most popular. Expect $50-$300+ for good viewing.

The Experience: Camping on the mountains the night before is legendary. Fans arrive early, set up tents, spend the night outdoors. When the cyclists pass, it's electric. The noise is deafening.
