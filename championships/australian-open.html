What It Is

The Year's Opening Major

The Australian Open is one of tennis's four Grand Slam championships. Held every January in Melbourne, it kicks off the tennis season. Top-seeded players arrive fresh from off-season preparation.

The Australian Open is known for its hard courts and exciting early-season tennis. Players are sharp. Matches are competitive. Cinderella stories happen.

Why it matters: Winning the Australian Open starts the year with a Grand Slam. Winning all four Grand Slams in a calendar year is the "Calendar Grand Slam" ‚Äî the rarest achievement in tennis.

Format

Tournament Structure

Draw Size: 128 Players

128 players in singles (64 men, 64 women). Seeded players get favorable placements. Qualifiers and wild cards also enter.

Match Format

Men: Best-of-five sets. Women: Best-of-three sets. Matches can last hours. Longest matches define the tournament.

Rounds Progress

First round through finals over two weeks. Round-robin qualifying earlier in the week. Main draw then dominates.

The Final

A single match between two champions. Hard courts mean fast-paced, aggressive tennis. The trophy is presented immediately after.

Culture

Australian Open Moments

Djokovic's Dominance: Won the Australian Open 10 times, more than any other Grand Slam. The tournament is his domain.

Serena's Dominance: Won multiple Australian Opens. Her matches are must-watch tennis.

Unique Moment (2021): Ashleigh Barty won in front of her home Australian crowd. The emotion was palpable.

Heat Concerns: Melbourne summers are hot. Play is sometimes suspended for extreme heat. This makes the tournament physically grueling.

Travel

Attending the Australian Open

Ticket Difficulty: Hard

Australian Open tickets are competitive. Expect $50-$500+ depending on match. Finals cost more. Australian fans have priority.

Day tickets are more accessible than reserved seating for finals.
