What It Is

Rugby's Ultimate Test

The Rugby World Cup is rugby union's international championship. Held every four years, 20 national teams compete in a knockout tournament. The sport is brutal, physical, and demanding.

Rugby is dominated by New Zealand (the All Blacks), South Africa (the Springboks), and Australia (the Wallabies). These three nations have won every World Cup since its inception in 1987.

Why it matters: In New Zealand, Australia, and South Africa, rugby is national identity. Winning the World Cup brings parades and eternal pride. The All Blacks' All Black jersey is the most iconic in rugby.

Format

How the Rugby World Cup Works

Group Stage

20 teams divided into groups. Each team plays every other team in their group once. Top teams advance.

Knockout Rounds

Quarterfinals, Semifinals, and Final. Sudden-death knockout format. Lose and you go home. No replays.

The Final

Two teams, 80 minutes of play plus extra time if needed. The match is brutal ‚Äî full-contact, tackling, scrums, and pure physicality. The winning nation celebrates for four years until the next tournament.

Mandela's Moment (1995): South Africa hosted and won the World Cup during apartheid's end. Nelson Mandela presented the trophy to the captain. Healed a nation.

The All Blacks' Dominance: New Zealand has won three World Cups (1987, 2015). The 2015 team is considered the greatest rugby team ever assembled.

Iconic Rivalries: New Zealand vs Australia (Bledisloe Cup). New Zealand vs South Africa. These nations define rugby excellence.

Travel

Attending the Rugby World Cup

Ticket Difficulty: Very Hard

Rugby World Cup tickets are precious. Finals can reach $500-$1,500+. Group matches range $50-$400.

New Zealand and South Africa matches sell out instantly due to passionate fan bases. Northern Hemisphere matches (England, France, Ireland) are slightly more accessible.
