What It Is

Golf's Championship of Champions

The Open Championship, often called The British Open in America, is golf's oldest major. First held in 1860 at Prestwick Golf Club in Scotland, it has been held every year since except during world wars.

The Open is played on links courses ‚Äî ancient, windswept courses with bunkers, rough grass, and unpredictable conditions. It's the toughest major to predict due to weather and course variance.

Why it matters: Winning The Open carries historical weight. Tiger Woods's three Opens. Jack Nicklaus's three. Ernie Els's two. Open champions are golf royalty.

Format

Tournament Structure

Links Golf

Played on traditional links courses. Wind is a factor. Bunkers are treacherous. Rough is thick. Conditions are brutal and unpredictable.

72-Hole Tournament

Four rounds of 18 holes. Lowest score wins. The tournament rotates among British and Irish courses.

Unpredictability

Unlike other majors, The Open winner is hard to predict because courses change. St. Andrews, Muirfield, Royal Birkdale ‚Äî each demands different strategy.

Culture

The Open's Greatest Champions

St. Andrews Magic: Winning The Open at St. Andrews is the ultimate achievement. Six different winners from 2000-2010 at St. Andrews created incredible moments.

Tiger at St. Andrews (2000): Won at golf's most historic course. Iconic moment in major championship history.

Ernie Els's Unpredictability (2022): Won The Open at age 52, becoming one of sport's oldest major winners.

British Pride: When British or Irish players win, the nation celebrates. It represents cultural heritage.
