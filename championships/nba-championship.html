NBA Championship

The pinnacle of professional basketball. 16 teams compete through the playoffs for the Larry O'Brien Trophy. The Finals are best-of-7, featuring the East and West champions.

Championship Overview

The NBA Championship is the ultimate prize in professional basketball. Established in 1946, the NBA Finals determines the best professional basketball team in the world. 16 teams (8 from the Eastern Conference, 8 from the Western Conference) compete through the regular season and playoffs to earn their spot.

The championship format consists of the NBA Finals, a best-of-7 series between the East and West champions, played in June. Teams play 4 home games and 3 away games in a 2-2-1-1-1 format.

Key Facts

  • Founded: 1947
  • Tier Rating: Tier 3 (Elite National)
  • Viewership: 30-50 million (domestic US)
  • Championship Format: Best-of-7 Finals
  • Most Titles: Boston Celtics (17), Los Angeles Lakers (17)
  • GOAT Moment: Michael Jordan, 1995-1998 three-peat with Chicago Bulls

The Finals Format

The NBA Finals pit the champions of the Eastern and Western Conferences against each other in a best-of-7 series. First team to win 4 games wins the championship and the Larry O'Brien Trophy.

Schedule: The series follows a 2-2-1-1-1 format, meaning the team with the better regular season record gets home court advantage. Games are typically played every 2-3 days in June. The entire playoff tournament runs from April through June.

How to Attend

Ticket Prices: Finals tickets range from $500 to $10,000+ depending on seating and game. Early playoff rounds are $100-$500. Standing room only in the cheapest sections.

Best Time to Visit: June during the Finals. Plan 7-10 days for a potential full series.

Budget: $3,000-$8,000 for a full Finals experience including travel, accommodation, and tickets.
