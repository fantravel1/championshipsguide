What It Is

International Basketball's Championship

The FIBA Basketball World Cup is international basketball's premier championship. Held every four years, 32 national teams compete to determine the world's best basketball nation.

The United States has historically dominated, winning four titles. But France, Spain, and other nations have proven competitive, with France reaching the finals multiple times.

Why it matters: Winning the FIBA World Cup validates national basketball superiority. It's rarer than Olympic gold because it happens every four years but features only the strongest basketball nations.

Format

Tournament Structure

Group Stage

32 teams divided into groups. Each team plays group matches. Top teams advance to knockout rounds.

Knockout Rounds

Round of 16, Quarterfinals, Semifinals, Final. Single-elimination. Lose and you're eliminated.

The Final

Two teams, one match, 40 minutes of basketball. First to score the most wins the world championship.

Culture

Iconic Moments

USA Dominance: Won multiple World Cups with elite talent including NBA stars.

France's Surge: Won in 1954 and 1960, and reached finals multiple times recently. Represents European basketball strength.

International Growth: Basketball globally is stronger than ever, making the World Cup increasingly competitive.

Travel

Attending the FIBA World Cup

Ticket Difficulty: Hard

FIBA World Cup tickets are competitive. Expect $50-$500+ depending on match. Finals cost more. Host country fans have priority.
