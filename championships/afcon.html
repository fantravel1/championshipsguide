What It Is

Africa's Greatest Football Tournament

The African Cup of Nations (AFCON) is Africa's premier football championship. Held every two years, it features 24+ African nations competing for continental glory.

AFCON is famous for passionate fans, dramatic matches, and intense national pride. Egypt, Cameroon, Ivory Coast, Ghana, and Senegal are traditional powerhouses. Upsets are common.

Why it matters: In African nations, AFCON victory is the pinnacle of sporting achievement. It can change governments' popularity, shift regional pride, and define national identity for years.

Format

AFCON Tournament Structure

Group Stage

24+ teams divided into groups. Each team plays group matches. Top teams advance to knockouts.

Knockout Rounds

Quarterfinals, Semifinals, Final. Single-elimination. Extra time and penalty shootouts determine winners.

Dramatic Finishes

AFCON is known for dramatic, emotional finishes. Penalty shootouts are common. Celebrations are massive.

The Final

Two African nations battling for continental supremacy. The match often features experienced, talented teams. Goals and drama define AFCON finals.

Culture

AFCON Passion & Tradition

Egypt's Dominance: Won AFCON seven times, the most of any nation. Egyptian fans are among world football's most passionate.

Cameroon's Strength: Known for physical, aggressive play. Multiple championships.

Senegal's Recent Surge: Won in 2021, reaching heights. Growing influence in African football.

Fan Culture: AFCON fans are incredibly passionate. National pride is maximized. Celebrations and mourning are intense depending on results.

Iconic Moments: Zambia's 1993 tragedy (plane crash killed team, they still made it to the finals). Egypt's penalty shootout victories. Cameroon's upsets. These moments define AFCON.

Travel

Attending AFCON

Ticket Difficulty: Moderate to Hard

AFCON tickets vary by host nation and match. Expect $30-$300+ for matches. Finals cost more. Home nation fans have priority and fill stadiums.

The tournament travels across Africa, so location impacts availability and price.
