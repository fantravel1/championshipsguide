What It Is

International Hockey Excellence

The Ice Hockey World Championship is professional hockey's international championship. Held annually in May, 16+ national teams compete in a tournament format for the world title.

Different from the Stanley Cup (professional club championship), the World Championship features national team competition. Players from different leagues and teams unite for their countries.

Why it matters: Winning the World Championship validates national hockey supremacy. Canada and Russia's dominance defines the rivalry. Sweden, Finland, and Czech Republic compete strongly.

Format

Tournament Structure

Group Stage & Knockouts

Teams divided into groups. Group winners advance to knockout rounds. Quarterfinals, Semifinals, Final. Single-elimination format after group stage.

Best-of-Three

Traditional best-of-three format in recent years, though this has evolved. Finals are single matches in modern tournaments.

Global Competition

North American teams (USA, Canada) compete alongside European teams (Russia, Sweden, Finland, Czech Republic, Switzerland). Best teams in the world assemble.
