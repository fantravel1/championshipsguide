What It Is

The Toughest Major Championship

The U.S. Open is golf's most brutally difficult major championship. Held annually in June, the USGA intentionally sets courses to punish golfers. Rough is thick. Fairways are narrow. Greens are lightning-fast.

Unlike the Masters (where the winner often shoots low scores), U.S. Open champions often finish at even par or worse. A score of +3 can win the U.S. Open. This is by design.

Why it matters: Winning the U.S. Open is golf's greatest achievement. Only the best can withstand the difficulty. Jack Nicklaus's four U.S. Opens. Tiger Woods's three. These define legacies.

Format

The U.S. Open Structure

Difficult Course Setup

The USGA chooses the most difficult possible course setups. Rough is grown intentionally thick. Fairways are narrow. Green speeds are 14+ on the stimp meter (extremely fast).

Qualifying

More open field than the Masters. Qualifying tournaments determine spots. Top golfers automatically qualify. This makes the U.S. Open more accessible than other majors.

72 Holes of Torture

Four rounds of 18 holes. Each hole is set to be as difficult as possible. Par becomes a celebration. Birdie is rare. The course wins more often than the golfer.

Playoff

Ties are resolved in an 18-hole playoff the next day. Or sudden-death overtime in recent years. The U.S. Open wants a decisive champion.

Culture

Iconic U.S. Open Moments

Winged Foot (2006): Geoff Ogilvy won at +5. The course was so difficult that positive scores were normal. This defines U.S. Open difficulty.

Shinnecock Hills (2018): The fastest greens ever set for a major. Phil Mickelson's par-putt rolled off the green. Chaos. The USGA went too far. Protesters complained.

U.S. Open Upsets: Unlike other majors, outsiders win the U.S. Open because the course gives everyone a chance to fail. Corey Conners. Webb Simpson. Dark horses thrive on brutal courses.

Travel

Attending the U.S. Open

Ticket Difficulty: Very Hard

U.S. Open tickets are expensive and limited. Expect $200-$1,000+ for tickets. Finals (weekend) are more expensive than early rounds.

General admission gets you walking privileges, but premium seating near greens is limited and expensive.
