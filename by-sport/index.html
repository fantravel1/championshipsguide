Championships by Sport

From football to Formula 1, every sport has its own hierarchy of titles. Find the championships that define your sport.

Football / Soccer

Football Championships

Football is the world's most popular sport, played in every country on Earth and watched by billions. Its championship hierarchy is the deepest and most layered of any sport — from the once-every-four-years pinnacle of the World Cup to the weekly drama of domestic leagues, continental club competitions, and historic cup tournaments. No other sport has championships represented across all five tiers of our rating system.

Tier 1

Football

FIFA World Cup

The single biggest sporting event on the planet. Held every four years, the World Cup crowns the best national football team on Earth and captivates billions of viewers across every continent. Expanding to 48 teams in 2026.

🌐 International Tournament 📅 Jun-Jul (every 4 years)
Tier 2

Football

UEFA Champions League

Europe's premier club competition brings together the best football clubs from across the continent in an annual battle for continental supremacy. Reformed to a 36-team league phase in 2024, followed by knockout rounds.

⚽ Club Competition 📅 Sep-Jun (annual)
Tier 2

Football

UEFA European Championship

The Euros bring together the best national teams in Europe for a month-long tournament every four years. With 24 teams competing across multiple host cities, it is one of the most-watched sporting events globally and second only to the World Cup in football prestige.

🌐 Continental Tournament 📅 Jun-Jul (every 4 years)
Tier 3

Football

English Premier League

The most-watched domestic football league in the world, broadcast in 189 countries to an estimated 4.7 billion people. Twenty clubs compete across a 38-match season from August to May, producing some of the most dramatic title races in sport.

⚽ Domestic League 📅 Aug-May (annual)
Tier 3

Football

La Liga

Spain's top football division and home to two of the biggest clubs in world football: Real Madrid and FC Barcelona. La Liga has produced more Ballon d'Or winners than any other league and is renowned for its technical, possession-based style of play.

⚽ Domestic League 📅 Aug-May (annual)
Tier 4

Football

Copa América

The oldest continental football tournament in the world, first held in 1916. South America's national teams compete every four years for continental supremacy. Home to footballing powerhouses Brazil, Argentina, and Uruguay.

🌐 Continental Tournament 📅 Jun-Jul (every 4 years)
Tier 1

Football

FIFA Women's World Cup

The pinnacle of women's football, held every four years since 1991. The 2023 tournament in Australia and New Zealand drew record-breaking global audiences and proved that women's football commands world-class attention and prestige.

🌐 International Tournament 📅 Jul-Aug (every 4 years)
Tier 3

Football

FA Cup

The oldest football competition in the world, founded in 1871. England's knockout cup is famous for its magic — giving lower-league clubs the chance to topple giants. The final at Wembley is one of the most iconic fixtures in world football.

⚽ Knockout Cup 📅 Aug-May (annual)
Basketball

Basketball Championships

Basketball is a truly global sport with deep roots in American culture and rapidly growing international participation. From the hardwood courts of the NBA to the global stage of the FIBA World Cup, basketball championships produce some of the most electrifying moments in sport.

Basketball

NBA Finals

The ultimate prize in professional basketball. A best-of-seven series between the Eastern and Western Conference champions determines the NBA's world champion. Home to the most iconic individual performances in team sport — from Jordan's flu game to LeBron's block.

🏀 League Final 📅 June (annual)

Basketball

FIBA Basketball World Cup

The global championship of basketball. Held every four years, national teams from around the world compete to be crowned world champions. The tournament also serves as a key qualifier for the Olympic Games and has been held since 1950.

EuroLeague

Europe's premier club basketball competition, featuring the top teams from across the continent. The EuroLeague Final Four is one of the most intense weekends in European sport, with passionate fanbases creating atmospheres that rival any sporting event in the world.

NCAA March Madness

The most dramatic tournament in American sports. Sixty-eight college teams compete in a single-elimination bracket that produces more upsets, Cinderella stories, and buzzer-beaters in three weeks than most sports produce in a decade. Over 100 million Americans fill out brackets annually.

🏀 College Tournament 📅 March-April (annual)

Basketball

WNBA Finals

The championship series of the world's premier women's professional basketball league. The WNBA has experienced a massive surge in popularity, with record viewership and attendance driven by generational talents and growing cultural significance.

🏀 League Final 📅 October (annual)

Basketball

Olympic Basketball

Basketball at the Olympic Games represents the pinnacle of international competition. With NBA stars representing their nations, Olympic basketball gold is one of the most coveted prizes in sport. The USA's "Dream Team" at the 1992 Barcelona Olympics remains one of sport's most iconic moments.

🏅 Olympic Event 📅 Jul-Aug (every 4 years)
Tennis

Tennis Championships

Tennis is defined by its four Grand Slam tournaments — the Australian Open, French Open, Wimbledon, and US Open. These four events are the pillars of the sport, and winning all four in a single calendar year (a "Calendar Grand Slam") is one of the rarest and most revered achievements in all of sport. Only a handful of players in history have accomplished it.

What Is a Grand Slam in Tennis?

In tennis, the term "Grand Slam" refers to the four most prestigious tournaments: the Australian Open (Melbourne, January), the French Open (Paris, May-June), Wimbledon (London, June-July), and the US Open (New York, August-September). Each is a two-week standalone tournament featuring men's and women's singles, doubles, and mixed doubles draws. Winning all four Grand Slams in a single calendar year is called a "Calendar Grand Slam" — achieved by only a select few players including Rod Laver (1962, 1969) and Steffi Graf (1988). Winning all four over the course of a career is called a "Career Grand Slam" — accomplished by legends such as Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, Novak Djokovic, and Serena Williams. The Grand Slams offer the most ranking points, the largest prize funds, and the most prestige in tennis.

Tier 2

Tennis

Wimbledon

The oldest and most prestigious tennis tournament in the world. Played on grass courts at the All England Club in London since 1877, Wimbledon is defined by its all-white dress code, royal patronage, strawberries and cream, and unparalleled tradition. It is the crown jewel of the Grand Slams.

🎾 Grand Slam 📅 Jun-Jul (annual)
Tier 2

Tennis

Australian Open

The first Grand Slam of the calendar year, held in Melbourne every January. Known as the "Happy Slam" for its relaxed atmosphere, scorching Australian summer heat, and passionate crowds at Melbourne Park. Novak Djokovic holds the record with 10 men's singles titles.

🎾 Grand Slam 📅 January (annual)
Tier 2

Tennis

French Open (Roland-Garros)

The world's premier clay-court tournament, held in Paris every May-June. Roland-Garros is widely considered the most physically demanding Grand Slam, with its slow red clay courts punishing players over best-of-five-set matches. Rafael Nadal's 14 titles here is one of sport's most extraordinary records.

🎾 Grand Slam 📅 May-Jun (annual)
Tier 2

Tennis

US Open

The final Grand Slam of the calendar year, held in New York every August-September. Arthur Ashe Stadium's 23,771-seat capacity makes it the largest tennis-specific stadium in the world. The US Open's electric night sessions under the New York lights produce an atmosphere unlike anything else in tennis.

🎾 Grand Slam 📅 Aug-Sep (annual)
Tier 3

Tennis

ATP Finals

The season-ending championship for the top eight men's singles players and doubles teams in the world. Held in Turin, the ATP Finals is a round-robin format followed by knockout semifinals and a final — a unique format that guarantees multiple matches between the best players alive.

🎾 Year-End Championship 📅 November (annual)
Tier 3

Tennis

WTA Finals

The premier year-end championship in women's tennis, featuring the top eight singles players and doubles teams on the WTA Tour. The WTA Finals brings together the best female players for a week of elite competition, with a rich history including legends like Serena Williams, Martina Navratilova, and Steffi Graf.

🎾 Year-End Championship 📅 November (annual)
American Football

American Football Championships

American football is the most popular sport in the United States, and the Super Bowl is the biggest single-day sporting event in the country. While the sport's competitive ecosystem is primarily North American, its cultural impact is enormous — the Super Bowl has become as much a cultural event as a championship game.

Tier 3

American Football

Super Bowl

America's biggest single-day sporting event is a cultural phenomenon — equal parts championship game, halftime spectacle, and national holiday. Over 110 million domestic viewers tune in annually. The Super Bowl is the culmination of the NFL season, pitting the AFC and NFC champions in a single winner-take-all game.

🏈 League Final 📅 February (annual)
Tier 3

American Football

College Football Playoff

The system that determines the national champion of NCAA Division I college football. Expanded to a 12-team format in 2024, the CFP crowns a champion through a knockout bracket of the nation's best college teams, played in massive stadiums before passionate student and alumni fanbases.

🏈 College Playoff 📅 Dec-Jan (annual)
Tier 5

American Football

Pro Bowl Games

The NFL's annual all-star event, reformatted in 2023 from a single game to a multi-day skills competition and flag football game. While the competitive intensity is lower than a regular season game, the Pro Bowl selection remains a significant individual honor for NFL players.

🏈 All-Star Event 📅 February (annual)
Cricket

Cricket Championships

Cricket is the second most popular sport in the world by number of fans, with a passionate following across South Asia, Australasia, the United Kingdom, the Caribbean, and Southern Africa. The sport has evolved from the traditional five-day Test format to the explosive T20 format, creating a rich championship landscape.

Tier 1

Cricket

ICC Cricket World Cup

The pinnacle of one-day international cricket. Held every four years since 1975, the Cricket World Cup determines the best national team in the 50-over format. The 2023 final between India and Australia at the Narendra Modi Stadium drew a live crowd of over 130,000 — the largest for any cricket match in history.

🌐 International Tournament 📅 Oct-Nov (every 4 years)
Tier 2

Cricket

ICC T20 World Cup

The global championship for cricket's shortest and most explosive format. The T20 World Cup features 20 teams competing in high-octane matches lasting approximately three hours each. The format's accessibility and entertainment value have made it hugely popular with newer cricket audiences worldwide.

🌐 International Tournament 📅 Jun (every 2 years)
Tier 5

Cricket

The Ashes

One of the fiercest and most storied rivalries in the history of sport. Played between England and Australia since 1882, The Ashes is a five-match Test series that alternates between the two countries every two years. The 2005 Ashes is widely regarded as one of the greatest sporting contests of the 21st century.

🏏 Bilateral Series 📅 Alternating (every 2 years)
Tier 3

Cricket

Indian Premier League (IPL)

The richest and most-watched cricket league on Earth. The IPL combines franchise-based T20 cricket with Bollywood glamour, massive broadcast deals, and the world's best cricketers competing alongside emerging Indian talent. It has transformed the economics and culture of world cricket since its launch in 2008.

🏏 Franchise League 📅 Mar-May (annual)
Tier 1

Cricket

ICC Women's Cricket World Cup

The pinnacle of women's international cricket. Held every four years, the Women's Cricket World Cup has grown dramatically in stature and viewership. The 2022 final between Australia and England at Hagley Oval drew global attention, showcasing the rapid growth and quality of the women's game.

🌐 International Tournament 📅 Feb-Mar (every 4 years)
Motorsport

Motorsport Championships

Motorsport encompasses a thrilling range of disciplines — from the high-tech aerodynamics of Formula 1 to the raw endurance of the 24 Hours of Le Mans, from the speed of MotoGP to the brutal terrain of the Dakar Rally. Each championship tests different skills at the absolute limit of human and mechanical performance.

Tier 2

Motorsport

Formula 1 World Championship

The pinnacle of single-seater motor racing. F1 races across five continents with a calendar spanning March to December, featuring the most technologically advanced cars and the best drivers in the world. The Netflix series "Drive to Survive" has propelled F1 to new heights of global popularity.

🏊 Season Championship 📅 Mar-Dec (annual)
Tier 3

Motorsport

24 Hours of Le Mans

The world's oldest active endurance race, held annually since 1923 at the Circuit de la Sarthe in France. Teams of three drivers push prototype and GT cars to their absolute limits across 24 hours of continuous racing, combining speed with strategy and mechanical reliability in the ultimate test of endurance.

🏊 Endurance Race 📅 June (annual)
Tier 3

Motorsport

Indianapolis 500

The "Greatest Spectacle in Racing" has been held at the Indianapolis Motor Speedway since 1911. Thirty-three cars compete in a 200-lap, 500-mile race around the iconic 2.5-mile oval, with over 300,000 spectators making it the largest single-day sporting event in the world by attendance.

🏊 Single Race 📅 May (annual)
Tier 4

Motorsport

Dakar Rally

The most grueling off-road endurance race on the planet. Originally run from Paris to Dakar, Senegal, the rally now takes place in Saudi Arabia across two weeks of punishing desert terrain. Competitors in cars, trucks, and motorcycles navigate thousands of kilometers of sand, rock, and dunes in a true test of survival.

🏊 Rally Raid 📅 January (annual)
Tier 3

Motorsport

MotoGP World Championship

The pinnacle of motorcycle racing. MotoGP riders compete on purpose-built prototype bikes capable of over 220 mph, leaning into corners at extreme angles that defy physics. The championship races across circuits worldwide and has a devoted following, particularly in Southern Europe and Southeast Asia.

🏊 Season Championship 📅 Mar-Nov (annual)
Golf

Golf Championships

Golf is defined by its four Major championships, which serve the same role in golf as the Grand Slams do in tennis. Winning a Major is the ultimate measure of a golfer's legacy, and winning all four over the course of a career — the Career Grand Slam — places a golfer among the all-time greats.

What Is a "Major" in Golf?

In golf, the four Majors are the most prestigious tournaments in the sport: The Masters (April, Augusta National, Georgia), the PGA Championship (May), the US Open (June), and The Open Championship (July, UK). These four events define golf careers — a golfer's greatness is largely measured by how many Majors they have won. Jack Nicklaus holds the all-time record with 18 Major victories, followed by Tiger Woods with 15. Winning all four Majors in a single calendar year has never been achieved in the modern era — it is golf's equivalent of the Calendar Grand Slam in tennis. Winning all four over the course of a career is called the "Career Grand Slam", accomplished by only five players in history: Gene Sarazen, Ben Hogan, Gary Player, Jack Nicklaus, and Tiger Woods.

Tier 2

Golf

The Masters

The most exclusive and revered tournament in golf. Held every April at Augusta National Golf Club in Georgia, The Masters is the only Major played at the same course every year. Its traditions — the green jacket, Amen Corner, the azaleas, the honorary starters — make it one of the most iconic sporting events in the world.

⛳ Major Championship 📅 April (annual)
Tier 2

Golf

The Open Championship

The oldest golf tournament in the world, first played at Prestwick in 1860. Known simply as "The Open," it is the only Major played outside the United States, rotating among iconic British links courses including St Andrews, Royal Liverpool, and Royal Troon. The Claret Jug is golf's most coveted trophy.

⛳ Major Championship 📅 July (annual)
Tier 2

Golf

US Open (Golf)

Organized by the USGA since 1895, the US Open is widely considered the toughest test in golf. The tournament is famous for its punishing course setups — narrow fairways, thick rough, and lightning-fast greens designed to test every aspect of a golfer's game. Winning the US Open demands precision, patience, and mental fortitude.

⛳ Major Championship 📅 June (annual)
Tier 2

Golf

PGA Championship

The second Major of the golf calendar, organized by the Professional Golfers' Association of America since 1916. The PGA Championship typically features the deepest field of all four Majors, with the top professionals from the PGA Tour and international tours competing for the Wanamaker Trophy.

⛳ Major Championship 📅 May (annual)
Tier 5

Golf

Ryder Cup

The most passionate and intense event in golf. Every two years, the best golfers from Europe and the United States compete in a three-day team match-play competition that transforms the traditionally individual sport into a fiercely nationalistic battle. The atmosphere at the Ryder Cup is unlike anything else in golf.

⛳ Team Competition 📅 September (every 2 years)
More Sports

More Championship Sports

The world of championships extends far beyond the major team sports. Here are the key championships across rugby, ice hockey, baseball, athletics, cycling, combat sports, and esports.

🏉 Rugby

Rugby Championships

Rugby union and rugby league feature some of the most physically demanding championships in sport. Key rugby championships include:

  • Rugby World Cup — Tier 1 The pinnacle of rugby union, held every four years since 1987
  • Six Nations — Tier 2 Annual European championship between England, France, Ireland, Italy, Scotland, and Wales
  • The Rugby Championship — Tier 3 Annual southern hemisphere championship between New Zealand, South Africa, Australia, and Argentina
🏒 Ice Hockey

Ice Hockey Championships

Ice hockey is one of the fastest and most physical team sports in the world. Key ice hockey championships include:

  • Stanley Cup — Tier 2 The NHL's championship trophy, contested through four grueling best-of-seven playoff rounds
  • IIHF World Championship — Tier 3 Annual international tournament featuring the best national teams in the world
  • Olympic Ice Hockey — Tier 1 When NHL stars compete for their countries, the tournament reaches a different level
⚾ Baseball

Baseball Championships

America's pastime has a rich championship tradition spanning over a century. Key baseball championships include:

  • World Series — Tier 2 MLB's best-of-seven championship series, played since 1903
  • World Baseball Classic — Tier 3 International baseball's premier tournament, featuring MLB stars representing their nations
  • College World Series — Tier 3 The NCAA Division I baseball championship, held annually in Omaha, Nebraska
🏃 Athletics

Athletics Championships

Track and field is the foundation of the Olympic Games and determines who is truly the fastest, strongest, and most enduring. Key athletics championships include:

  • World Athletics Championships — Tier 1 The global championship of track and field, held every two years
  • Diamond League — Tier 3 The premier annual series of elite track and field meetings across 14 global venues
  • World Marathon Majors — Tier 3 The six most prestigious marathons: Tokyo, Boston, London, Berlin, Chicago, and New York
🚲 Cycling

Cycling Championships

Professional cycling is one of the most physically demanding sports in the world, with its Grand Tours lasting three weeks across thousands of kilometers. Key cycling championships include:

  • Tour de France — Tier 2 The most prestigious cycling race on Earth, three weeks across the French countryside
  • Giro d'Italia — Tier 4 Italy's Grand Tour, famous for its dramatic mountain stages in the Dolomites
  • UCI Road World Championships — Tier 3 The annual world championship where the winner earns the coveted rainbow jersey
🥊 Combat Sports

Combat Sports Championships

Boxing, MMA, and martial arts have a long and storied championship tradition, from the golden era of heavyweight boxing to the modern dominance of the UFC. Key combat sports championships include:

  • UFC Championship Bouts — Tier 3 The premier championship events in mixed martial arts, crowning champions across weight classes
  • Boxing World Title Fights — Tier 3 Title fights sanctioned by the WBA, WBC, IBF, and WBO across 17 weight divisions
  • Olympic Boxing & Judo — Tier 1 Olympic gold in combat sports represents the highest honor in amateur competition
🎮 Esports

Esports Championships

Competitive gaming has exploded into a billion-dollar industry with massive global audiences. Esports championships fill arenas and draw millions of online viewers. Key esports championships include:

  • League of Legends World Championship — Tier 3 The biggest event in esports, drawing 100M+ peak concurrent viewers
  • The International (Dota 2) — Tier 3 Famous for its community-funded prize pool that has exceeded $40 million
  • CS2 Major Championships — Tier 4 The most prestigious tournaments in competitive Counter-Strike

Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to common questions about the world's most important championships across every sport.

The FIFA World Cup is universally considered the biggest championship in football (soccer). Held every four years, it is the most-watched sporting event on the planet, drawing billions of viewers worldwide. National teams from over 200 FIFA member associations compete in a multi-year qualification process for the right to play in the final tournament (expanding to 48 teams in 2026). In the ChampionshipsGuide tier system, the FIFA World Cup is rated Tier 1: Pinnacle, the highest possible ranking for any championship in any sport.

At the club level, the UEFA Champions League (Tier 2) is the most prestigious competition. At the domestic league level, the English Premier League (Tier 3) is the most-watched league in the world, broadcast in 189 countries. Football is the only sport with championships represented across all five tiers of our rating system.

At the international level, the FIBA Basketball World Cup is the biggest championship in basketball, rated Tier 1 in the ChampionshipsGuide system. It is the global tournament where national teams compete every four years to determine the world champion. Olympic basketball also holds Tier 1 status due to the global reach of the Olympics and the prestige of an Olympic gold medal.

At the professional club level, the NBA Finals are the most prestigious basketball championship (Tier 2), crowning the best team in the world's premier basketball league through a best-of-seven series every June. The NBA Finals command massive global audiences and have produced some of the most iconic moments in sporting history — from Michael Jordan's flu game to LeBron James's historic comeback from 3-1 down in 2016.

Teams qualify for the UEFA Champions League primarily through their domestic league performance. The top-finishing clubs in each European league earn automatic entry, with the number of spots depending on the league's UEFA coefficient ranking.

For example, the top four finishers in the English Premier League, La Liga, Serie A, and the Bundesliga all qualify directly for the league phase. Lower-ranked leagues may send one or two teams, who often must navigate preliminary qualifying rounds.

The defending Champions League title holder also receives automatic entry for the following season. Since the 2024-25 season, the competition features a reformed league phase with 36 teams playing eight matches each against different opponents, followed by a knockout playoff round for teams finishing 9th-24th, and then the traditional knockout rounds leading to a single-match final.

The Champions League is the most prestigious club football competition in the world and is rated Tier 2 in the ChampionshipsGuide system.
