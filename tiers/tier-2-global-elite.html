Tier 2: Global Elite

The world's premier annual and biennial championships. Hundreds of millions of viewers. Elite worldwide prestige. Held every year or every two years. These are the championships that happen regularly enough to follow a career's trajectory.

Definition

What Defines Tier 2?

Tier 2 championships are the most prestigious annual and biennial competitions in sport. While not held as rarely as Tier 1, they maintain elite status through consistent global viewership in the hundreds of millions and deep prestige within their sport. These championships happen every year (or every other year), allowing fans and athletes to build regular traditions.

Characteristics

Why Athletes Pursue Tier 2

Regular Opportunity: Unlike Tier 1 events that happen every 4 years, Tier 2 championships happen annually or biennially. You can realistically win multiple times in your career.

Career-Defining: Winning multiple Tier 2 championships (e.g., Federer's 8 Wimbledons, Tiger's 5 Masters) creates immortal legacies more impressive than single Tier 1 victories.

Global Prestige: While not quite Tier 1, Tier 2 victories are globally significant. Winning Wimbledon or the Champions League makes you a global icon.

Accessible Viewership: Because these happen annually, fans can follow careers more closely and build traditions around championships.
