The Methodology

How We Rate Championships

The Championship Tier System‚Ñ¢ is built on a foundation of five critical evaluation criteria. Each championship is assessed on these dimensions independently, then combined to determine its tier placement. This methodology is sport-agnostic ‚Äî we don't favor football over tennis, or cricket over hockey. Instead, we measure objective significance across universal metrics.

The five criteria are weighted equally but measured on a 1-10 scale for each championship. Tiers are assigned based on the aggregate score and qualitative assessment of global significance.

The Five Tiers

Championship Classifications

Tier 1: Pinnacle ‚Äî The rarest global events with billions of viewers. FIFA World Cup, Olympic Games, Cricket World Cup, Rugby World Cup, FIBA Basketball World Cup, World Athletics Championships. These transcend sport.

Tier 2: Global Elite ‚Äî Annual or biennial events with hundreds of millions of viewers. Champions League, Wimbledon, Masters, U.S. Open Tennis, PGA Championship, NBA Finals, Stanley Cup, Tour de France, Formula 1, and more. Elite worldwide prestige.

Tier 3: Elite National ‚Äî Strong championships with deep regional or national significance. Super Bowl, World Series, Copa America, UEFA Euro, AFCON. Massive within their scope but primarily national or continental.

Tier 4: Prestigious ‚Äî Well-established championships with strong traditions. FA Cup, Giro d'Italia, Wimbledon Doubles, Grand Slam qualifying rounds. Significant prestige but smaller global audience.

Tier 5: Historic & Cultural ‚Äî Championships with deep roots or growing significance. National championships, specialized sport events, emerging championships. Complete the landscape of competitive sport.
